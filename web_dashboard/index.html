Illustrating Key Variables of Insurance Costs

This chart plots out several variables to determine their impact on health insurance costs. Each variable is assigned a number based on the study data, and the closer the number is to the number 1 the more is it correlated with increased insurance coverage costs. From this we find that age, body mass index, and smoking have the most profound impact on costs.

How does this apply to a single person?

We can see that there is variation in the insurance costs from one individual to another based on their own unique circumstances. Our research verifies this but lacks a direct and practical application to an individual.
